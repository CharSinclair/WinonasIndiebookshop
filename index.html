Winona's Indie Bookshop

Our Brand

Winona’s Indie Bookshop is grass roots project to create a hub for indie writers to promote their work. We are currently in the community building phase of the project to gain online and local (Vegas) support. We often see BIPOC, LGBTQIA+, and disabled people turn to independent forms of publishing due to the continued oppression of marginalized people. Our quest is simple. Create space for diverse indie writers to easily connect their work with future readers through community building events, an online community, and a YouTube podcast.

Our Current Offerings:

  1. Blog Style Bookclub on Discord
  2. Books & Brews (Vegas Local Events)
  3. Check out our podcast Interviews With Indie Authors

About the Owner

Previously, I worked as a Speech-Language Pathologist and helped students with disabilities improve their communication skills. It was at this job I finally realized I was neurodiverse. Due to burn out, I quit in December of 2023 and had to take care of my mental health. After some rest and existential crises, I started writing a poetry book called, “Lost in the Desert” in April 2024. This journey is what eventually led me to attempt growing a bookish social media account from scratch and realizing that indie writers are better together. It was beautiful the way I eventually found my people. I had discovered that the indie writer community was so diverse and always willing to help out through reviews, shout outs, and even crowdfunding. Once I found a few authors I enjoyed, it opened the door to learning about other writers in the community through their support of one another online. When founding my podcast and community, it was important to me that my identity as a queer, neurodiverse, Chicana influence the type of space I was creating. The algorithms and search engines so clearly favored our white straight counterparts so I made it my mission to create a third space for us to share our work, algorithm free.
-Winona Veil (She/Her)
